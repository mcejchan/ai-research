# Vercel's Guillermo Rauch on AI and the Future of Coding - Ep. 47

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Shift from Coding to Product Delivery
**Quote:** "I don't think I would identify today even as a coder even though that's what I obsessed about for years since I'm like 10 years old."  
**Why it matters:** This highlights a fundamental shift in the developer landscape where the focus is moving from writing code to delivering high-quality products and experiences. As AI tools evolve, developers are encouraged to think more about the end goals rather than the implementation details.

### 2. Embracing Meta Skills
**Quote:** "What are the meta skills that are not as easily replicated by machines that you should still nurture?"  
**Why it matters:** As AI takes over specific coding tasks, developers need to cultivate higher-level conceptual thinking and problem-solving skills. This shift emphasizes the importance of understanding how different components of technology fit together, rather than just focusing on coding.

### 3. The Importance of Dogfooding
**Quote:** "We dog food our tools; we put product experiences before tools."  
**Why it matters:** This principle ensures that the products developed are genuinely useful and meet user needs. By using their own tools, companies can identify gaps and improve their offerings, leading to better products and a more engaged development team.

## Practical Tips & Procedures

### Building a Culture of Innovation
**What it does:** Encourages a company-wide ethos of creativity and product development.  
**Steps:**
1. Foster an environment where employees can experiment with new ideas and technologies.
2. Implement regular feedback loops with customers to refine products.
3. Encourage cross-disciplinary collaboration among teams to enhance creativity.
**Quote:** "You want to always put the product first, not the framework first."  
**When to use:** This approach is beneficial during product development phases, especially when exploring new technologies or market needs.

## Important Warnings/Risks
- **Over-reliance on AI:** "When things are specific skills, machines tend to take them over over time." - This highlights the risk of developers becoming too dependent on AI tools, potentially leading to a decline in fundamental coding skills and problem-solving abilities.
