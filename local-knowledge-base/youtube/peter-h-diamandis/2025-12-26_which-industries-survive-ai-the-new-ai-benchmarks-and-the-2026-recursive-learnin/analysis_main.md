## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Need for Industry-Specific AI Benchmarks
**Quote:** "I think the problem is though... we need thousands of new narrow benchmarks to capture maybe every labor category, every industry vertical."  
**Why it matters:** The current focus on broad AI benchmarks is insufficient for industries that require specific metrics. Tailored benchmarks will help businesses assess AI's impact on their unique operations and guide effective implementation.

### 2. The 2026 Disruption Deadline
**Quote:** "We're going to see the largest disruption ever in 2026 from companies that don't make this change."  
**Why it matters:** Companies that fail to adapt to AI technologies by 2026 risk becoming obsolete. This urgency highlights the importance of proactive strategies for integrating AI into business models.

### 3. The Role of Expertise in AI Implementation
**Quote:** "Do you have the expertise in-house to do this?"  
**Why it matters:** Many companies lack the necessary skills to implement AI effectively. This gap necessitates either hiring specialized talent or partnering with external providers to ensure successful AI integration.

## Practical Tips & Procedures

### Focus on High-Impact Use Cases
**What it does:** Identifies key areas where AI can significantly improve business outcomes.  
**Steps:**
1. List two to three critical business functions that could benefit from AI (e.g., customer service, inventory management).
2. Develop a proof of concept for one of these functions.
3. Consider partnering with a third-party vendor that ties compensation to results.
**Quote:** "Make sure you get to a pilot stage in that one or two... not a strategy document."  
**When to use:** When initiating AI projects in medium to large companies.
