# Steve Yegge's Vibe Coding Manifesto: Why Claude Code Isn't It & What Comes After the IDE
**Channel:** Latent Space  
**URL:** https://youtu.be/zuJyJP517Uw?si=ZsiqokaeaAKS0otd

## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Resistance to Change
**Quote:** "There is a demographic that is the most affected by that. Their identity is the most tied up with the way that they work."  
**Why it matters:** Senior engineers with 12-15 years of experience often resist new methods like vibe coding and AI integration, feeling threatened by the shift. This resistance can hinder innovation and adaptation in the tech industry.

### 2. Productivity Disparities
**Quote:** "The performance difference is like 10x any way that you measure it."  
**Why it matters:** Companies are noticing stark productivity differences between those who adopt AI tools and those who do not. This disparity can lead to significant challenges in performance reviews and team dynamics.

### 3. The Shift from IDEs
**Quote:** "If you're still using an IDE to develop code by January 1st, you're a bad engineer."  
**Why it matters:** Yegge emphasizes the need to move away from traditional IDEs and embrace new coding methods that leverage AI, suggesting that those who resist this change may fall behind in their careers.

## Practical Tips & Procedures

### Embrace AI Coding
**What it does:** Transition from traditional coding methods to AI-assisted coding.  
**Steps:**
1. Dedicate time to learn how to effectively use AI tools for coding.
2. Practice with AI for at least 200 hours to build familiarity and trust in its capabilities.
3. Shift your mindset to rely on AI for coding tasks rather than traditional methods.
**Quote:** "You have to spend 200 hours with it."  
**When to use:** When transitioning to AI-assisted development to enhance productivity and efficiency.

### Use Agent Orchestration
**What it does:** Manage multiple AI agents effectively to streamline coding tasks.  
**Steps:**
1. Set up an orchestration dashboard to monitor agent activities.
2. Utilize notifications from agents to stay updated on their progress.
3. Implement a system for agents to communicate and coordinate tasks among themselves.
**Quote:** "You're going to walk in in the morning and be like, 'Yo, so how are things do?'"  
**When to use:** In environments where multiple AI agents are deployed for coding tasks.

## Tools & Technologies Mentioned
- **Cloud Code:** A tool for AI-assisted coding that has not yet gained widespread adoption. It requires reading and understanding complex diffs, which can be challenging for many engineers.

## Important Warnings/Risks
- **Over-reliance on AI:** "The LLM at any moment can stab you in the back." - It's crucial to maintain oversight and not fully trust AI without understanding its limitations, as it can lead to significant errors in production environments.
