## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Importance of Understanding AI
**Quote:** "When you go a little bit outside of this bubble, you find that people really don't know what AI is about."  
**Why it matters:** This highlights the gap in AI understanding between industry leaders and the general public. As AI continues to evolve, it's crucial for companies to bridge this knowledge gap to ensure effective communication and implementation of AI technologies.

### 2. The Evolution of AI Roles
**Quote:** "We started with prompting... and there's been roles for prompt engineers."  
**Why it matters:** The emergence of new roles like prompt engineers signifies the evolving landscape of AI. Understanding these roles can help organizations better prepare for the future workforce and the skills needed to leverage AI effectively.

### 3. Fine-Tuning as a Key Strategy
**Quote:** "Fine-tuning is the way to go."  
**Why it matters:** Fine-tuning models allows organizations to customize AI outputs to better meet their specific needs. This approach can lead to improved accuracy and efficiency, making it a critical strategy for businesses looking to harness AI effectively.

## Practical Tips & Procedures

### Fine-Tuning AI Models
**What it does:** Fine-tuning adjusts AI models to improve performance based on specific data and requirements.  
**Steps:**
1. Start with prompting to evaluate the model's performance.
2. Use retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to ground your data.
3. If results are satisfactory, proceed to fine-tuning.
**Quote:** "Don't go to fine-tuning right off the bat... you still have to figure out how you can do it by prompting it first."  
**When to use:** Use this process when you have a clear understanding of your data and evaluation framework, and you're ready to enhance your AI model's performance.
