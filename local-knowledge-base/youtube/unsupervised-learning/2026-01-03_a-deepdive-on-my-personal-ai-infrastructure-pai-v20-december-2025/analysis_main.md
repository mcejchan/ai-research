## Key Takeaways

### 1. The Importance of AI Augmentation
**Quote:** "The main reason is because I just wanted to get better overall at everything that I do."
**Why it matters:** This highlights the necessity of using AI as a tool for personal and professional development. As jobs evolve, leveraging AI can help individuals enhance their skills and adapt to changing work environments.

### 2. Scaffolding vs. Models
**Quote:** "Scaffolding is more important in my opinion than the model."
**Why it matters:** This emphasizes the significance of having a robust framework (scaffolding) to support AI applications, rather than solely relying on the latest AI models. A strong scaffolding can lead to better outcomes and more efficient processes.

### 3. Code Before Prompts
**Quote:** "If I have anything that I can do in code, I do it in code first."
**Why it matters:** This principle advocates for prioritizing deterministic code over AI prompts when possible. It ensures consistency, reduces costs, and enhances control over the outcomes of AI applications.

## Practical Tips & Procedures

### Building Your Own AI System
**What it does:** Provides a framework for creating a personal AI augmentation system.
**Steps:**
1. Identify your core interests and what you want to improve.
2. Develop a clear understanding of how technology can save you time.
3. Start building with a focus on deterministic code before integrating AI prompts.
**Quote:** "You can't just start building... who are you? What do you care about?"
**When to use:** Use this process when starting to implement AI into your personal or professional workflows.

### Specifications, Tests, and Evals
**What it does:** Establishes a structured approach to ensure consistency and effectiveness in AI applications.
**Steps:**
1. Create specifications for what you want to achieve.
2. Develop plans based on those specifications.
3. Write tests to evaluate the effectiveness of your implementation.
4. Write the code based on the tests and specifications.
**Quote:** "First you create specs, then you create plans, then you write tests, then you write code."
**When to use:** Use this method when developing new AI skills or applications to ensure they meet desired outcomes.
